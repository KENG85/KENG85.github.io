Single Player Case Study

Pro-Day training for a Strong Safety. This athlete lost a significant amount of weight during the season. Our goal was to gain weight while maintaining speed. By tracking weight x power, and finding the root cause for performance, we were able to gain 27lbs in 12 weeks. He posted a 4.48s 40yd time at his Pro-Day.

'BFS' is Limited by Power

The traditional training strategy of "Bigger, Faster, Stronger" only holds true for today's athlete so far as the ahtlete's power and speed are maximized. There are going to be some compromizes when it comes to gaining mass, but speed cannot be one of them.


We tracked player power out-put via an average of the 4-Jump test using the Just Jump mat. This provided both an ongoing index of player CNS fatigue as well as a relative gague of speed as an index of power. We tracked the 4-Jump to monitor recovery and power-to-mass ratios to find that sweet spot in the BFS continuum.

GAMER™ Performance

Examining lifestyle variables that impact performance, we determined that motivation to train was highly dependent upon nutrition leading up to the training session(.72). Additionally, there was a strong negative correlation between life stress and nutrition (-.86) and life stress and motivation to workout (-.63).


Our GAMER™ strategy for player performance was to implement stress management techniques and emphasize a strategy to ensure optimal nutrition. See correlational data below.
