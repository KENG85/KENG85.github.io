Team Analytics

When looking at large-team data, breaking down insights by position group can be useful for a number of reasons. Coaches often utilize different training strategies for players of different positons which can have different effects on sub-populations that might not be noticeable when looking at whole-team data.

Position Group GAMERs™

We see different root cause trends emerge by position groups. What makes one position-group tick, isn't necessarily the same thing as another group. Coaches can use this information to talor motivational strategies and and provide targeted interventions to sub-groups for improved performance. We might find intuitive trends, like quarterbacks have the lowest perceived life stress among all position groups. This is a key finding as there is a lot of research on impaired decision making under stress.


Root-cause correlations for sub-population performance variables are identified and we work collaboratively with position coaches to develop GAMER strategies to improve performance trends at the position-group level.

Other Metrics

We work collaboratively with coaches and performance staff to track, analyze and visualize data that is relative to their coaching or perofrmance strategies.


Trends in weight, strength etc. can be ustilized to gain insight to postion-group norms. Coaches can then use this data to find out where recruits fall when making roster decisions etc.
