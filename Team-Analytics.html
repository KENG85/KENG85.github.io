Team Analytics

When looking at large-team data, breaking down insights by position group can be useful for a number of reasons. Coaches often utilize different training strategies for players of different positons which can have different effects on sub-populations that might not be noticeable when looking at whole-team data.

Behavioral Models Differ By Position Group

We see different root cause trends emerge by position groups. What makes one position-group tick, isn't necessarily the same thing as another group. Coaches can use this information to talor motivational strategies and and provide targeted interventions to sub-groups for improved performance.


We can further examine these subgroups to identify any root-cause correlations for performance. We might also find intuitive trends, like quarterbacks have the lowest perceived life stress among all position groups. This is a key finding as there is a lot of research on impaired decision making under stress.

GAMER™ Performance

Examining lifestyle variables that impact performance, we determined that motivation to train was highly dependent upon nutrition leading up to the training session(.72). Additionally, there was a strong negative correlation between life stress and nutrition (-.86) and life stress and motivation to workout (-.63).


Our GAMER™ strategy for player performance was to implement stress management techniques and emphasize a strategy to ensure optimal nutrition. See correlational data below.
